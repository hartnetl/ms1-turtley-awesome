Welcome

If you've made it this far, I'm assuming you know who we are and what we're about. But if not, let me tell you.
This is a website dedicated to raising awareness for sea turtle conservation. Of the 7 species of sea turtle on Earth, six are of concern to the IUCN. The seventh isn't even classified. These animals are faced with man-made threats every day, from fishing nets to climate change. These beautiful animals are at risk, but they don't have to be.
If everyone pledged to make small changes, we could drastically improve the health of our oceans and give not only these animals, but every animal, a better chance at survival. So why not start today?

General conservation information

Sea turtles have been around for millions of years. 120 million to be (relatively) precise, which means they were swimming around while dinosaurs roamed the earth. They haven't changed too much since then, and despite surviving those millions of years, their populations have been decimated in recent times.
Even though hunting sea turtles is largely illegal now, poaching still occurrs and huge fishing trawlers deplete the oceans of turtles' prey, or accidentally catch the turtles themselves. A high percentage of those released still die. As global temperatures rise, the balance of male to female sea turtles is shifting out of balance, as sex is determined by nest temperatures. As coastal areas are developed, nesting beaches become unsuitable for turtles to lay in. Even if they lay a nest, hatchlings which typically emerge at night are easily disoriented by the bright lights which leads to many deaths. Plastic pollution is another serious cause of turtle death.
But hope is not lost. By making a few small changes in our day-to-day lives, we can undo the damage we have done to let these animals return to their previous abundance.
